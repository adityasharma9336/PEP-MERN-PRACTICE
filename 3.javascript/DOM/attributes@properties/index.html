DOM: Attributes and Properties





In a small village nestled between blue mountains, there lived a young girl named Tara. The village was beautiful during the day, but at night, it became a place of absolute darkness because the villagers were poor and could not afford expensive oils for lamps. Families would retreat into their homes as soon as the sun dipped below the horizon, leaving the streets silent and cold. One evening, Tara found a piece of translucent paper and a discarded candle stub near the market and decided to create something special. She sat by her window and carefully folded the paper into the shape of a delicate star, placing the lit candle inside. She stepped outside and hung the glowing star on a low-hanging branch of the village's oldest tree, where its tiny, warm light danced in the center of the square. One by one, the neighbors peered out of their windows, drawn by the soft, flickering amber glow that pierced the gloom. Old Mr. Gupta, who usually grumbled about the dark, came out with a small jar and a wick to join her. Inspired by Tara, other children began bringing scraps of colored paper and old jars to build their own lanterns. By the next night, ten more lights were swinging from the branches, and the darkness that once felt heavy was now filled with a tapestry of color. People began to stay out later, sharing stories and laughter under the warm, artificial stars that they had built together. The village square transformed into a place of community and safety rather than a place to be feared. Merchants from nearby towns heard of the "Village of Light" and began to visit to trade, bringing newfound prosperity to the residents.
